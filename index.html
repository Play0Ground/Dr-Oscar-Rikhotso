ABOUT DR. Oscar Rikhotso

Oscar Rikhotso is a distinguished lecturer in the Department of Environmental Health at the Tshwane University of Technology (TUT) in Pretoria, South Africa. With over a decade of experience in occupational health, Oscar has developed a deep expertise in occupational hygiene, particularly in the context of South Africa's industrial sectors. He holds a Doctor of Philosophy in Science from TUT and has been actively involved in advancing the understanding of occupational health risks, especially related to noise exposure in various industries.

As a Certified Occupational Hygienist with the Southern African Institute for Occupational Hygiene (SAIOH), Oscar has contributed significantly to the field through research, policy advocacy, and academic mentorship. His work often focuses on noise risk assessments, hearing conservation programs, and the broader implications of occupational hazards on worker health and safety. His research has been widely published in prestigious journals, addressing key issues such as noise control in manufacturing, occupational health regulations, and the social responsibility of companies in managing health risks.

Oscar is passionate about improving workplace health standards in South Africa and beyond, aiming to bridge the gap between regulatory frameworks and their practical applications in industry. His commitment to this cause is evident through his extensive publication record, which includes impactful articles in journals like Safety, Health SA Gesondheid, Annals of Work Exposures and Health, and Sustainability.

Outside of academia, Oscar collaborates with industry stakeholders to implement effective occupational health risk management strategies, ensuring safer work environments for employees across various sectors.
